## System Context

Among NLIs, notifications are seen as a way to get more information about the products and features on apps installed.

We have seen that many apps in India use notifications as a valid growth tactic and some of our past notifications have seen user raves. In countries such as IN, we have also  seen that notifications do tend to have higher CTRs than in the US.

- Users check phones **50–100+ times/day**
- ~**50% of notifications are ignored**
- Majority of sessions last **under 30 seconds**
- Messaging platforms dominate information flow in India

For NIUs, the journey from initial onboarding to long-term usage is rarely straightforward.

The very first interaction with Maps is a "make-or-break" moment: it either builds the confidence to keep exploring or introduces friction that leads to immediate drop-off.

Because these users lack prior context with digital mapping, they often find the learning curve steeper than expected.

As a result, the platform's core, high-value features—the ones that actually drive retention—often feel too complicated and intimidating during early sessions.

**Implication:** You don’t design for exploration. You design for **instant comprehension + action**.

## Notification Strategy

**Key Insight:** Notifications are **entry points, not features**. If ignored, the system fails before the UI is even seen.  
From **Event-based alerts** to **Context-aware triggers**.

### The Framework

- **Timing (When):** High-interruptibility windows (commute, idle time, post-work)
- **Context (Why now):** Location relevance, Time sensitivity
- **Clarity (What action):** Immediate comprehension (under 2 sec scan)

**Definition:** Notifications = compressed decisions delivered at the right moment.

## Hyperlocal Content System

**Key Insight:** Users don’t consume feeds. They **continuously evaluate relevance**.
**Impact (qualitative):** Users spend less time figuring out relevance.

### Conceptual mocks (content feed)

- Content feed sorted by proximity (“Near you” prioritization)
- Card with distance + time metadata visible upfront
- Live/ongoing event highlighted with temporal badge
- Social signal indicators (activity, comments) influencing ranking
